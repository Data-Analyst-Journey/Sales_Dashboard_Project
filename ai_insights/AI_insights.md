### 1. THREE KEY BUSINESS INSIGHTS

- **Pareto power concentration** – Top 20% of customers drive **84% of revenue**, with the #1 customer alone contributing **22.8%**. This creates efficiency but also extreme dependency.

- **Day-of-week imbalance** – **Friday generates 19.6%** of weekly revenue (2x Thursday's 9.6%). Thursday is the clear underperformer, not Monday.

- **High-value customer behavior** – Top customers average **10.3 invoices** and **207 units per purchase** (bulk, repeat buyers). Single-purchase customers (37%) and products (39.4%) signal low retention and narrow inventory turns.

---

### 2. TWO SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS

- **For customer retention (top 20%)** – Launch a **tiered loyalty program** with volume discounts, dedicated account support, and early access to new products. Prioritize retention of Customer 31 (22.8% of revenue) and Customers 58 & 13 (another 20.6% combined).

- **For customer acquisition (reduce dependency)** – Run a **Thursday-specific promotion** (e.g., "Thursday bulk order discount" or free shipping) to lift the weakest day and attract new mid-tier customers. Target product bundles of slower-moving items (39% purchased once) to cross-sell.

---

### 3. ONE RISK THAT NEEDS IMMEDIATE ATTENTION

**Customer concentration risk** – Losing just the top 3 customers would wipe out **43% of revenue**. The business is one decision (or competitor offer) away from a major revenue crash.

---

### 4. ONE SIMPLE METRIC TO TRACK NEXT MONTH

**% of revenue from top 3 customers** – Aim to reduce from 43% to <38% by growing the next 10 customers (e.g., customers ranked 4–13).
